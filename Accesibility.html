Accessibility

Planning phase

In building our website, accessibility was a primary focus to make sure it's usable for everyone. We combined automated testing with manual checks to ensure our design choices went beyond what automated tools can catch. We aimed for high contrast between text and background colors, following WCAG guidelines, to support users with low vision or color blindness. Instead of a fully white background, we used soft colors in different sections to reduce eye strain. We used relative units like rem and em for font sizes, allowing users to resize text easily. To make the site adapt well across devices, we used fluid units like vh, vw, and percentages instead of fixed pixels. This approach helps our site look good on any screen size. We added descriptive alt text for all images so that screen readers can convey the visual content to users who can't see it. Our heading tags h1, h2, h3 are also structured in a logical order to improve navigation for screen readers. Below, you'll find our initial sketch, which is the first image. This serves as a rough plan outlining how we intend to begin building our website. Additionally, you'll find the final sketches, represented by the last three images.
