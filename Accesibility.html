Accessibility

Planning phase

In building our website, accessibility was a primary focus to make sure it's usable for everyone. We combined automated testing with manual checks to ensure our design choices went beyond what automated tools can catch. We aimed for high contrast between text and background colors, following WCAG guidelines, to support users with low vision or color blindness. Instead of a fully white background, we used soft colors in different sections to reduce eye strain. We used relative units like rem and em for font sizes, allowing users to resize text easily. To make the site adapt well across devices, we used fluid units like vh, vw, and percentages instead of fixed pixels. This approach helps our site look good on any screen size. We added descriptive alt text for all images so that screen readers can convey the visual content to users who can't see it. Our heading tags h1, h2, h3 are also structured in a logical order to improve navigation for screen readers. Below, you'll find our initial sketch, which is the first image. Reports

We continuously tested our website using Lighthouse to ensure accessibility and usability. Early in the process, Lighthouse identified that our headings were not in proper hierarchical order. To address this, we reorganized the structure by arranging headings in a logical sequence and consistently reusing h2 and h3 tags where necessary. We also made several visual enhancements to improve clarity and user experience. For example, we adjusted the link colors to provide clearer feedback when hovered over or clicked. Additionally, we increased the spacing between target links, making navigation easier and reducing the likelihood of accidental clicks. In the final phase, we ran a comprehensive Lighthouse analysis one last time. Based on its feedback, we improved the background color contrast across the site to ensure better readability, particularly for users with visual impairments. These adjustments collectively made our website more accessible, user-friendly, and aligned with web accessibility best practices.
